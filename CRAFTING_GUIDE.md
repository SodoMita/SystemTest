# System Looting — The Operator's Crafting Field Guide

*The council sits in the dark. Only the terminal screens glow.*

**Carmack** pulls up a schematic on the main display.

"We keep talking about the *loop*. Scavenge, build, survive. But if a new agent opens their inventory and sees nothing but a blank grid, the loop is broken before it starts. So we write it down. We make it teachable."

"Every item in this system has a parent. Nothing comes from nowhere. Scrap becomes ingots. Ingots become blades. Blades keep you alive long enough to build the Core. And the Core wins the match."

**Maura** leans back. "Then let's write the manual in blood and neon."

---

## 🎒 How to Craft

1. Open your inventory with **I**.
2. The **Crafting** tab is already selected.
3. Pick a category: **Salvage**, **Equipment**, **Tactical**, or **Objective**.
4. Click the green recipes you can afford. Items are consumed from your main inventory and the result is placed back in it.
5. Greyed-out recipes mean you are missing ingredients. Search by name if the list gets long.

> **Tip:** Right-click the 3D agent preview in any tab to open the **Character** screen — team status, lives, and currently equipped uniform are all there.

## 📦 Where to Find Salvage

- **Loose Items** (`sl_modebase:item_pickup`) are glowing objects scattered on the map. Right-click them to claim scrap.
- **Loot Crates** are larger containers placed by map builders; open them to see what was left behind.
- The **Monster Master** has his own economy, but the living agents live on what they can carry.

---

## ⚠️ Safety Notes

- Recipes in the **Objective** category are the most expensive. Do not waste components on side gear unless your team is ready to defend.
- The **Objective Core** can only be placed near your own beacon. Trying to deliver it elsewhere will warn you, not win the game.
- If the Core is placed while no match is running, it sits there and waits — another team can still steal the moment.
